Chapter 6

I woke to the sound of hushed voices and the soft clink of glass. Two mages sat beside my bed, one from the Council and one a palace healer. My father was there, standing tall with arms crossed. My mother sat at the foot of the bed, holding my sister's hand. All three looked tired, but not surprised.

Apparently, a maid had found me asleep for far too long and notified my mother. When she checked on me herself and couldn’t wake me, they brought in the healer. The diagnosis was clear: I was out of Veil. That alone was strange enough to summon the Council mages who had stayed behind after the duel.

We lived just a short walk from the royal palace. As general of the royal army, my father had been granted a residence in one of the stone houses reserved for high command. It wasn't luxury, but it was close. Close enough that the moment anything was wrong, they could bring the Council to our door.

When I opened my eyes, the healer leaned forward first. But it was the Council mage who spoke.

"You are stable now."

My throat was dry. "What happened?"

"You were out of Veil," he said.

That struck a chord. My eyes widened. I sat up too quickly. "Did I manifest?"

Hope filled me like air in a drowning chest.

The mage raised an eyebrow. "Yes."

Relief nearly made me laugh. If I had manifested, I could be disqualified. I could be saved.

I turned toward the Council mage, the same one who had stood in the court yesterday. "Then I’m no longer a candidate, right? I can be removed?"

He shook his head.

The hope collapsed.

He sighed and sat down beside me. "You did manifest, yes. But not with a disqualifying affinity. On the contrary, you manifested Stillmark."

Stillmark.

My head spun.

"Stillmark is a rare affinity," he continued. "It channels calm and inner stillness. It stabilizes the Veil around you and within others. It is highly compatible with Soulshape support."

I stared at him. "But... it’s a feminine affinity."

He didn’t flinch. "Yes."

No one else in the room spoke.

I turned to my mother. "Did you ever think I might get Stillmark?" My voice shook on the question.

She looked just as shaken as I felt and shook her head. "Never."

When a noble child is born, there are tests, expensive, uncertain things that hint at what affinities the child might develop. They're never accurate, just vague probabilities. But rich families pay for them anyway. The results usually list a handful of possible paths. Most children grow into one of them. But not always.

Mine had not included Stillmark. The test had listed possible affinities like Ironmind, Windsway, or even Watersoul. But Stillmark wasn’t there. Not even as a remote possibility.

The Council mage folded his hands. "Some scholars believe the state of the body at the time of manifestation plays a role. Not the whole story, but a piece. Most people manifest during times of stress or emotional instability, extreme fear, fatigue, pain. The Veil responds by stabilizing the imbalance."

He paused. "In your case, your mind and body were overwhelmed. It’s possible Stillmark manifested to bring you back to center."

That made my stomach twist.

So it was my fault?

If I hadn’t collapsed. If I had gone to train instead of sulking. If I had fought, distracted myself, done anything but lie in bed feeling sorry for myself, would I have gotten a different affinity?

The mage saw the shift in my face.

He raised a hand. "Most scholars, including myself, believe that’s unlikely. The affinity reveals itself as it always would. Stillmark was already in you. The moment simply opened the door."

He smiled gently. "It makes your fate even more beautiful. Now your soul, your magic, and soon your body, they will all match. You were always meant to become her."
