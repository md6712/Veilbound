Chapter 12

Rinya, that is my name. Kaelira named me. And I liked it.

We had been waiting, just behind the entrance to the great hall. Soon, Father and the king would arrive to escort us. I hated the whole idea, being escorted like a trophy.

Mother tried to reassure me, saying this was expected of women. We must be accompanied by a man. She even teased me gently, saying I’d stolen her man for the evening. Of course, she was joking. She had no issue with it. In fact, she was to be escorted by Sir Knight Alvis, a distant cousin of hers and a longtime family friend.

That made it worse.

Sir Alvis was a role model to me. A high-ranking royal guard. A knight who had earned acclaim in multiple campaigns. I had once dreamed of becoming like him. Now, I was just a girl in a gown, waiting to be debuted.

Pathetic.

Father and the king arrived a few minutes later. They were deep in political discussion, something they clearly continued after a prior meeting. But as soon as they saw us, Kaelira and me, they stopped.

Whatever they had been talking about vanished.

Their eyes widened. They were stunned.

Kaelira and I looked down. We curtsied together, alongside our mothers. Around us, those in attendance followed suit, either bowing or curtsying.

I envied the ones who could simply bow. It was easier. Simpler. Cleaner. Curtsying still felt so degrading to me, no matter how many times Serenya told me it wasn’t.

But I held my pose.

Because that’s what was expected.

Because that’s what Rinya version of me did now.

The king stepped forward first. He approached Kaelira and embraced her. It wasn’t formal, it was warm. Protective. As a father should be.

Then my father turned to me and wrapped me in his arms. I felt like a tiny child again in his embrace, dwarfed by his frame. But also safe. So safe.

The king turned to face us both.

"You have done something no sword could match," he said. "A sacrifice this realm will never forget. Your names will be carried in honor."

He glanced at my father, then back to me. "Your family is already revered. But tonight begins a new chapter. Tonight, we honor you, Rinya."

Then the hall doors opened.

One by one, couples stepped forward into the light of the main hall. Names were called by the court herald.

"Lady Myrienne of House Maeren, escorted by Sir Alvis of the Royal Guard."

Mother looked serene, composed as ever. She gave me a soft smile before she took his arm. Sir Alvis, tall and steady, bowed to the crowd as they entered, greeted by gentle applause.

Next was Serenya.

"Lady Serenya of House Maeren, escorted by Prince Eryndor of the Royal House."

She beamed, graceful as ever, and Prince Eryndor was every bit the knight he was rumored to be. Strong. Silent. Stoic.

Then came the Crown Prince.

"His Grace Prince Edren of the Royal House, and Princess Alerya of House Tibbon, Princess-Consort of Elaria, First Daughter of the Highland Throne of Everis"

They entered into more warmth. They were already beloved.

Then it was my turn.

"Lady Rinya of House Maeren, escorted by Lord Pasvil Maeren, Supreme General of the Royal Army of Elaria."

My heart pounded. Father gave me his arm with a proud, gentle nod.

We stepped into the main hall.

The light hit my face. I squinted. The room was vast, balconies layered above, filled with nobility. Musicians played soft strings. The floor shimmered with polished stone.

I walked beside my father, trembling, but upright.

As we walked, Father whispered, "Don’t be afraid, love. We are in this together."

It wasn’t usual. He often used that language with Serenya, not with me or my brother. But tonight, he said it to me.

People stood as we passed.

Then, the final names were announced.

"Princess Kaelira of the Royal House, escorted by His Majesty King Thalen, and Her Majesty Queen Avarielle."
