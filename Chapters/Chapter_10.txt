Chapter 10

We were escorted to our chambers. I had my own.

I knew I was supposed to live in the palace for the time being, but I didn’t expect to be given my own chambers, right beside the ones that now belonged to the prince. I mean. The princess. Kaela. It was hard to adjust.

My sister and mother joined me.

I was drenched in sweat from the ritual, exhausted and dazed. I wanted to change clothes. I asked for my old ones, but my sister just laughed.

"You can’t wear those anymore," she said, shaking her head. She turned to the maid and asked her to bring a chemise, stays, petticoats, and a soft shift, undergarments proper for a lady.

The maid returned quickly with a stack of garments, soft, delicate, clearly made for women. I stared at them, half in awe, half in confusion. I hadn’t even known things like this existed.

But I was too tired to argue. The maid and my sister helped me into them.

Everything felt strange.

Every movement felt off, unfamiliar. When they tried to remove my ritual gown, the hem caught on my chest, and I gasped. One of my breasts shifted with the fabric. It was so sensitive. I flinched.

The maid apologized at once.

I turned away, covering myself. My cheeks burned. It was embarrassing.

My sister laughed. "It’s okay," she said. "Women see each other naked all the time, especially sisters. Don’t be shy."

Then, as if remembering something, she added, "Though in some kingdoms they’re stricter. But here? You’ll get used to it."

I didn’t answer.

Everything still felt too new.

I wanted to see myself in a mirror, but it was too much effort to walk to the mirror.

The feeling of the cloth was too sensitive, my skin was, I assumed. Once I was in my new soft clothes, I just lay down. My sister told me to rest. I was too drained.

As soon as I put my head down, I drifted into sleep.

---

I woke up alone. At first I was dizzy, but then another need hit me, I had to relieve myself.

I tried to get out of bed. The hem of my dress slipped across my legs. It was delicate, lacy.

My fingers brushed over the fabric, so soft, so fine, and I felt a jolt of shame. This was what I wore now?

I shook the thought away. There were more pressing concerns.

I turned toward the door, intending to find the closest bathhouse, only to realize there was a private toilet room built into my chambers. That was a small relief. I didn’t want to wander the halls.

A soft snore caught my attention, my maid was asleep in the chair beside the bed.

I crept to the washroom and tried to do what I’d always done. But it didn’t work the same way. I stared, frustrated. Then, sighing, I sat down instead. That felt wrong. It felt vulnerable. But necessary.

When I finished, I reached for the cloth set aside for cleaning. It was more complicated than I expected. Nothing was where it used to be.

Somewhere between confusion and frustration, I must’ve made a noise.

My maid stirred. Then stood. "Let me help, milady," she said, and stepped into the doorway.

I flushed with embarrassment, about to protest, but she had already grabbed a warm cloth and knelt beside me. She didn’t flinch. Her hands were practiced, careful.

"It’s normal," she said. "Happens all the time with noble girls the first time they wear full gowns. You're not the first. Won’t be the last."

I couldn’t meet her eyes. But I let her help.

It was the first moment I truly realized: I had crossed into another world.

Once done, I moved to the standing mirror near the window.

I expected to see some ugly, awkward version of myself, a boy poorly pretending to be a girl.

Something misshapen. Wrong.

Instead, I was hit hard by the truth.

I was fully a girl. And not just a girl, a beautiful one.

My figure was soft, curved in all the places I never imagined for myself. The underclothes shaped me clearly. My skin glowed faintly, still flushed from rest. My black hair fell in gentle waves past my shoulders. My face was delicate, symmetrical, almost noble-looking. My eyes… they were green. Bright. Deep.

I stared, stunned. She stared back.

That was me.

I felt my legs go weak. A wave of vertigo hit me, and I feared I might fall. I grabbed the chair beside the window and sank into it, just in time. My maid stirred and, seeing me unsteady, hurried to my side.

"Do you need water, milady?" she asked.

I nodded.

As she stepped away, I remained still, staring at the mirror. I couldn't stop thinking, who was that girl? Was that really me?

When she returned, I took the cup in both hands. The water helped. Slowly, I looked up at her. She seemed about my age.

"What’s your name?" I asked.

"Mirna," she replied with a smile.

"It’s nice to meet you," I said. I hesitated. Then tried to say my own name.

"I’m... Rynn."

But the moment it left my lips, I regretted it. It didn’t match the reflection. It didn’t feel right.

And yet, what else could I say? I didn’t want to be called anything else. But calling myself Rynn now felt like I was betraying the memory of who Rynn had been. The strong, loyal boy. A son to a proud general.

I could never be him again. But I didn’t want to forget him either.

That was the dilemma.

An hour later, my sister, Serenya, and my mother returned.

I was still in my undergarments when they arrived. They entered with another maid, arms full of gowns.

Mirna and the other girl moved to place the dresses carefully into the wardrobe.

Serenya gave me a look and said, "Take these for now. We are close enough in size, and the seamstress will measure you before the day is out."

But then my mother added, "There’s no time to waste. We have to get you dressed. It’s time to meet the king."
