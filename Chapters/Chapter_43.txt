Chapter 43

Everything collapsed at once, and I fell with her.

When Kaelira’s strength gave way, mine followed without resistance. I tried to catch her, but the Veil surged between us with such force that it stole the last of my balance and clarity.

Her fear and mine folded together until I could not separate them, and the world tilted hard enough that the stone floor seemed to rise to meet us.

Darkness took me before I understood that we had both fallen.

I do not know how long I remained there. Time lost its meaning in that empty space. What brought me back was not a voice or a touch, but the Veil itself.

At first, it brushed against me lightly, like a distant tide. Then it grew stronger and more deliberate, surrounding me with a steady presence that guided rather than forced. It reached into whatever depth I had fallen into and drew me upward with patient control.

A Heartsense mage was working on us.

I could feel the difference at once. This Veil did not belong to Kaelira, and it did not belong to me. It was external, focused, and precise, woven with the kind of discipline that comes from long practice. Whoever she was, she knew exactly how much to give and where to place it.

My breath returned first, slow and uneven. Sight followed after, blurred and reluctant.

When I opened my eyes, the world came back in fragments. Light hovered above me, then faces began to form, still uncertain at the edges.

The first I recognized was my sister.

Serenya leaned over me and wrapped her hand firmly around mine, as though she feared I might slip away again if she loosened her grip.

Relief settled in my chest, quiet but real.

Then I saw who stood beside her.

Therrin.

He was closer than I had ever seen him in such a moment, his attention fixed entirely on me, concern plain and unguarded in his eyes.

Heat rose to my face before I could stop it.

I did not understand the reaction fully. Perhaps it was because I lay there weakened, unable to hold myself with any dignity, or perhaps it was because he saw me in a way I was not prepared for. Either way, I did not want him to witness it.

I closed my eyes again at once, too quickly to pretend it had been natural, and hoped that I had done it in time.

I knew I had not.

Still, I kept them closed for a moment longer, steadying my breath while the Veil continued to move around me.

When I opened them again, I forced myself to look past them.

Kaelira was beside me, and she was already being helped upright. Her movements were slow and unsteady, as though she had not yet fully returned to herself.

A lady knelt near her, her robes marked with the sigils of Heartsense. Light gathered at her hands as she worked, guiding the Veil with careful precision as she stabilized Kaelira’s breath and awareness.

Beside her, Lady Maerel extended her own Veil outward in steady waves. She did not lead the healing, but she supported it, reinforcing the work with measured control that spoke of training and discipline.

The combined effect surrounded us both. It was not overwhelming, but it was unmistakable. The Veil moved like a current shaped by their will, holding us steady as we returned.

I became aware then of where we were.

The court had not dispersed.

If anything, it had drawn closer.

Nobles stood around us in a wide circle, watching with open curiosity or poorly hidden judgment. Even in this state, we were still something to be observed.

At the center of that attention stood the King and the Queen.

They were no longer seated upon their throne. They had risen and moved nearer, their presence deliberate and commanding. The King’s expression held concern tempered by control, while the Queen’s remained rigid and guarded.

Hands reached for me, helping me sit and then stand. My legs trembled, and the world tilted again, though it did not collapse this time.

Kaelira was brought to her feet as well. She swayed slightly, and without thinking, I leaned toward her. She did the same. The bond between us steadied what our bodies could not.

The King spoke then, firm enough to silence the murmurs that had begun to rise again.

“That is enough.”

The hall quieted immediately.

“Take them to their chambers,” he ordered. “They require rest.”

There was no hesitation from those around us.

Then his gaze settled on Kaelira, and though his tone remained controlled, there was intention behind his next words.

“Let her rest,” he said. “She must remain well for the days to come.”

He paused, allowing the weight of his authority to settle before continuing.

“My son has returned, and there will be cause for celebration. We will hold gatherings and feasts, and in due time we will prepare for the wedding.”

The words settled over the court like a final judgment.

Not a suggestion. Not a question.

A declaration.

My thoughts struggled to follow, still slowed by the lingering effects of the Veil and the shock of everything that had just occurred.

We had not been asked. The decision had already been made.

We were guided away then, hands firm but careful as we were led through the parted ranks of the court. I did not resist, but I did not fully understand either. The path blurred as we moved, corridors passing without shape or memory.

Only when I reached my chamber did the world begin to settle again.

The doors closed behind me, and the quiet returned. My maid, Mirna, rushed to my side at once, steadying me before I could fully lose my balance. The touch of the Heartsense faded, leaving only the echo of its steadiness behind.

I stood there for a long moment, breathing slowly as my thoughts finally caught up.

The King’s words repeated in my mind with growing clarity.

"My son has returned. We will prepare for the wedding."

Understanding followed.

Not Jaelric.

Not Maedric.

But Prince Aelyon! Judew! The man who had traveled beside us. The man who had hidden his name. The man who had stepped forward before the entire court and claimed Kaelira as his bride. And the King had not only allowed it. He had declared it.

I felt something tighten deep within my chest, something that was not quite fear and not quite anger. It was something far more complicated. Because everything had shifted again, and this time I understood with painful clarity that nothing would return to what it had been. There would be no undoing this. There would only be what came next.
