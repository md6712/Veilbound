Chapter 15

Inside the princess's chamber, I curtsied to both the queen and Princess Kaelira. The motion still felt awkward, my legs stiff, balance uncertain, but I did it.

Kaelira was dressed in a gown of soft peach and ivory, layered with translucent silk that shimmered like morning mist. Her hair was braided down one side, pinned with tiny pearls.

There was a shyness in her expression that mirrored my own, a reluctance in the way she held her arms close, as if her body still wasn’t her own. I understood. In a way no one else could, I understood.

There was comfort in seeing her.

We were bonded, after all. Not just by circumstance. Not even just by ritual. But by Veil itself. I was her Veilbound. She was the only one in this world who could look at me and see the same strangeness reflected back.

I went to her without thinking. I took both her hands.

She did something only girls seemed to do without hesitation, she hugged me, both arms tight and soft around me. I returned the embrace. I didn’t think. I just held her back.

Something happened then that took us both by surprise. Our chests, those strange, soft mounds we still hadn’t fully adjusted to, pressed against each other. The contact was unexpectedly sensitive. I saw her flinch slightly, and I knew I had done the same. It startled us, reminded us of everything new, everything fragile. But then, just as quickly, something shifted.

A warm calm spread through me. A pulse, not pain, not shock, but something like magic.

Familiar. Reassuring. The Veil between us stirred, and the bond we shared shimmered quietly.

We didn’t pull apart. Instead, we held on tighter. Because in that moment, it helped. It soothed. We felt steadier in each other’s arms than in our own skin.

Somewhere behind us, I heard the unmistakable sound of noblewomen softly gasping. "Awww,"

someone murmured.

Only then did I notice we weren’t alone. My mother was present, standing near the window. My sister, Serenya, stood beside her. And another woman, elegant, with a falcon-pin clasping her shawl, stood by the queen’s side. I didn’t know her name, but I had seen her often in the queen’s shadow.

The queen gestured for us to sit. I found a spot beside Kaelira, still holding her hand loosely.

She clapped once, gently, and instructed the maids to bring chilled wine and fruit.

When the maids left, the queen turned her gaze to us, expression calm but firm. "There are things you must understand before you depart."

We nodded in unison.

"First," she said, looking to Serenya, "your sister will join you, Lady Rinya. And so will Prince Eryndor."

My heart lifted slightly at the news. Not just because Serenya would come, but because Prince Eryndor would be with us. Someone strong. Someone steady.

"Tradition requires that a princess be escorted to her new household by a male of her family’s line. Kaelira cannot be handed over by a servant or even a noble escort. It must be kin."

She folded her hands.

"But beyond tradition, your father and the king have agreed on a second necessity: security.

Prince Eryndor’s personal guard will form the rear of your convoy. His knights are loyal, battle-tested, and less... ornamental than the honor guard you met yesterday."

That made sense. It wasn’t just ceremony anymore. It was survival.

I felt Kaelira squeeze my hand.

The queen leaned forward slightly. "You are not symbols anymore. You are players in a game that eats symbols alive."

Kaelira turned to the queen then. "But Mother... I am afraid.

How can we survive this? This cruel prince I am to be wed to, how am I supposed to stand beside him? I hate the idea of him being close to me."

I understood her. I myself didn’t want any man getting close to me with that kind of intention. The idea of intimacy with any man, it made my stomach turn. It felt disgusting.

Wrong. I couldn’t imagine letting anyone near me like that.

She placed her hand gently over hers. "Time will help, my dear."

"How are we supposed to survive? Prince Eryndor will leave us soon and we’ll be alone. What if they kill us as soon as the knights return home? What if we’re just... left there?"

The queen shook her head. "They cannot do that, dear one, your safety becomes their burden the moment you step onto their soil. If anything happens to you, they won’t just be at war with us, but with the Council and every neighboring kingdom that observes its laws."

I nodded slowly. "But they could still keep us prisoners."

"Yes," she acknowledged, "they could. But not all of the knights will return. The ones your father named yesterday in the hall, those loyal to you, will remain. They were chosen for more than a ceremony. They are there to protect you, for as long as you remain in Veylan."

The idea that our friends would stay with us helped. I had thought they were only to accompany us there, part of the escort, nothing more. But now, knowing they were to remain by our side? That gave me something I hadn’t felt in days: hope.

They could protect us.

In my current form, I had no idea how much I could protect myself. I hadn’t trained like this. I hadn’t *lived* like this. I could guess, and it wasn’t much. Not compared to them. Not compared to strong men with swords and armor.

Yes. I was no longer a strong man.

The queen continued. "Serenya and Lady Maerel will accompany you both as well."

I blinked. Maerel?

"Serenya will return after a few days," she clarified. "But Lady Maerel will remain. She will guide you. Teach you what must be learned, how to move, how to speak, how to be received."

I was puzzled. I had seen the woman often beside the queen, but never knew her name. When the queen said it aloud, everything clicked. Lady Maerel, the widow of Commander Halvar, one of the heroes lost in the last war. She was known throughout the court as grim, unsmiling. There were stories about her, how she rarely spoke, how even senior nobles bowed a little deeper in her presence. Yet despite her sternness, she was beautiful. Striking, really. Even older than my mother, she still carried herself with poise that left no room for pity. Rumor once called her the Pearl of Elaria.

The queen looked at me directly. "She has the same affinity you do, Rinya."

I straightened slightly. Stillmark. That changed everything.

Shortly after, the maids returned with a silver tray of tea, wine, and small cakes arranged like tiny sculptures. They placed everything with ceremonial care, and the scent of rose and honey filled the room. I expected silence or more planning, but something shifted. The women began to talk.

Not about politics or strategy. But about gowns. Hair oils. Dyes for lips. A new fabric being traded from the western isles. It was... ladies’ talk.

It felt too foreign to me. And judging by Kaelira’s silence, I wasn’t alone.

I looked around the room. Maids moved quietly between us, refilling cups and adjusting cushions. But what struck me was the six of us seated together, my mother, my sister, Lady Maerel, the queen, Kaelira, and me. Six women in delicate, elegant gowns, their skirts spilling like waves over polished chairs and cascading onto the floor in a spill of color: plum, seafoam, rose gold, ivory, and mine, lavender.

I was one of them.

The air felt so distinctly feminine, perfumed with tea and laughter and silk rustling. But somehow... it wasn’t as awful as I feared. It was strange, yes. But also warm. Not cruel.

Some part of me, quiet, tired, let itself rest in that warmth, just for a moment.
