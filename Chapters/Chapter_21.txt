Chapter 21

Our first stop brought us to Greenhall, a castle set on the eastern ridges beyond the capital's reach. It belonged to Lord Halric of House Enmar, uncle to Joval. The caravan was to stay the night and depart again with the first light. We arrived just before sunset, a perfect hour when the light turned everything gold.

As our carriages rolled through the outer gate and into the lush gardens, I couldn’t help but take in the beauty of the estate. It wasn’t massive, but it was cultivated, every hedge shaped, every path clean, the grass still wet from an afternoon misting. Vines bloomed across the stone walls in trained patterns, and flowering trees lined the walk leading up to the keep. It was the sort of place that felt touched by gentle hands.

The lord himself stood at the center of the path, flanked by his household. Lady Anire, tall and graceful, wore pale violet and carried the air of a woman who knew how to command a room with little more than her gaze. Beside her stood two sons, one near Kaelira’s age and the other younger, no more than fifteen. And at her side, quiet and poised, stood a daughter, perhaps seventeen, with soft eyes and perfect posture.

As the royal carriage drew to a stop, I leaned slightly toward the window, just enough to catch the sight of Prince Eryndor dismounting with calm precision. His companions followed, one by one, their movements practiced from a hundred rides. Among them were some of my oldest friends, now transformed by the armor and distance of duty.

A moment later, one of the prince’s knights approached our cabin door. With a gloved hand, he opened it and offered his arm in quiet ceremony. One by one, he helped each of us down with grace, starting with Kaelira. She moved like someone balancing between practiced poise and something more fragile beneath. Serenya followed, light on her feet, her gown catching the amber light of sunset. Elsin took the knight’s hand with her usual elegance, a flicker of flirtation not far behind.

I was last. The knight offered his hand as he had for the others, and I took it with care, trying not to let my steps falter on the stone path below. My skirts brushed against the soft grass as I stepped out into the golden light of Greenhall. Lord Halric stepped forward. He bowed first to Prince Eryndor, then to Kaelira.

"Your Highnesses," he said with reverence, "welcome to Greenhall. It is an honor beyond measure to host the Crown and the future Princess of Veylan beneath my roof."

Eryndor nodded. "You honor us with your welcome, Lord Halric."

Kaelira inclined her head. "Thank you, my lord. It’s a beautiful estate."

Lord Halric straightened. "May I present my family. My wife, Lady Anire of House Marrin. My sons, Tyven and Roran. And my daughter, Evelyne."

Each family member offered a courteous bow or curtsy. Evelyne's gaze lingered just a second longer on Kaelira, as if searching for something, before she smiled softly and stepped back.

There was a pause, only a breath, but in it lived the tension of noble formality. A test of grace. And Kaelira passed it. She smiled with practiced warmth, offering a nod to each name.

"We thank you all for opening your home to us," she said. Her voice did not shake.

I remained behind in the second carriage, watching the exchange unfold from within the shadows. My fingers curled in my lap. These were the types of families I used to visit beside a prince or with my family. Now, I was introduced as something else. As someone to be seen differently. Not a guard, not a court shadow. Not a son of a general, not a young lord. A lady.

After the formalities were exchanged, Prince Eryndor took a moment to introduce each of us.

"Allow me to present the ladies of the royal entourage," he said. "Lady Serenya of House Maeren and her sister, Lady Rinya, the Veilbound Companion of Princess Kaelira; Lady Elsin of House Talyra; and Lady Maerel of House Varent."

With each name, the members of Lord Halric’s household bowed or curtsied once more. I felt the weight of their gazes on me when my name was spoken, Veilbound. A word that now meant I was something sacred, or something strange. Perhaps both.

Lord Halric’s gaze settled on Serenya first. "We are honored to host you, Lady Serenya. You are soon to be part of the royal family, and it brings us joy to see such a union between houses." He then turned to me. "And to have the Veilbound herself among us, your presence is no less significant."

As he looked to each of us in turn, we lowered into curtsies. I was not yet comfortable with the act. My knees remained uncertain, and grace still felt like something I was learning, but I did my best. It was expected.

The prince and Lord Halric exchanged a few brief words of congratulations on the engagement.

Lady Anire placed a hand lightly on Serenya’s arm. "We are truly happy for you."

Then Lord Halric’s gaze moved to Elsin and finally to Lady Maerel. "And of course, we welcome your companions with equal grace."

Then Lady Anire stepped forward. "You must be tired from the road. Come, let us show you to your chambers."

And so we were guided through the arched entry hall and into the heart of the keep. Kaelira was led to a suite in one of the tower wings, far grander than the rest, no doubt prepared for royalty. Meanwhile, Serenya, Elsin, and I were directed toward the southern guest wing. The rooms were still spacious, each with wide windows and soft bedding, and enough luxury to soothe any travel aches.

My maid, Mirna, joined me shortly after I arrived, carrying the smaller satchel I’d left behind in the carriage. She set it quietly by the low chest at the foot of the bed and gave me a soft smile. For the first time that day, I let out a breath I hadn’t realized I was holding.

Not long after, one of the house staff arrived to inform us that, after a short refreshment, dinner would be served in the main hall.

Mirna immediately set to work, helping me out of my travel garments. Her fingers were quick and gentle, unfastening the laces and smoothing out the fabric with practiced care. She brought over a basin of fresh water and a cloth, guiding me through the process of washing away the dust of the road. Then, she helped me into a gown more fitting for a courtly dinner, soft blue silk with a high collar and sleeves edged in lace. It wasn’t as extravagant as what Kaelira might wear, but it made me feel like I belonged in this new skin, if only for the evening.

What never stopped catching me by surprise was how soft my body had become. Every time I was naked, even for a fraction of a second, I felt exposed in a way that startled me. Every inch of me was soft to the core, especially in certain places. That softness felt fragile, unfamiliar, almost unreal.

After getting changed and a bit of rest, one of the house maids arrived to accompany Elsin and me to the main hall. Lady Anire greeted us at the door and drew us into the hall. We were soon joined by many others, Lord Halric, their elder children, Prince Eryndor, and Serenya. The knights were already gathered as well, including the men who had once been my closest companions.

Only Kaelira had yet to arrive.

Elsin and I were led to where Serenya, the prince, and Lord Halric were speaking. The lord offered us seats, but Serenya declined with a smile. "We’ve been sitting all day," she said.

"A few minutes of standing will do us good."

A few moments later, Kaelira entered.

Her entrance turned a few heads, though she carried herself as if she didn’t notice. The moment she stepped into the hall, it felt complete. She joined us with a nod.

Shortly after, Prince Eryndor excused himself to speak with his knights, and the lord and lady of the house moved to attend to the final preparations with the staff. For a brief, quiet moment, the three of us remained, Serenya, Kaelira, and I, standing in a small pocket of calm.

Then, a familiar voice called out.

"Lord Cale," Serenya said with surprise. "I didn’t see you. You came from behind."

He gave a small smile as he approached. "Greeting, ladies," he said, then looked toward Kaelira and offered a respectful bow, though something in his body language felt... off.

Polite, yes, but not quite sincere. It was the kind of gesture one gives out of obligation, not warmth. "Princess. I hope I’m not intruding."

I looked at Kaelira. She looked back at me.

"You are not, Lord Cale," she said.

It was strange. We never needed this level of formality to address each other before. Things were obviously different now. What unsettled me more was the bizarre tension in the air. I couldn't name it, but it settled beneath my skin like a splinter. I felt uneasy, though I wasn’t sure why.

What was striking now was how tall Cale seemed. He had always been around our height, but now I had to tilt my chin to meet his eyes. Maybe we hadn’t changed size at all, but standing in these skirts and soft slippers, he felt taller.

"I just wanted to say," he said, turning slightly toward Kaelira, "that I’m honored to serve you. I’ll do my best to protect you from any danger that may come."

Such nice words. Only why did they feel so hollow? Polished. Practiced. I didn’t believe him.

He then looked at me. That smirk of his, it was the same one he used to wear when teasing me during sparring drills, only now it pulled at my nerves in a new way. "You look ravishing, Lady Rinya," he said, the words slow and deliberate, like he wanted them to sting just enough to stay with me. His eyes flicked across my figure in a way that was rather lewd. There was no effort to hide it, and it made my skin crawl.

I must have been in his place. He lost the game, even after he used his magic; I won.

I gave him a short smile and said, "Thank you, Lord Cale," out of politeness. That was what was expected from ladies. I wasn’t an expert at these things, but I had seen enough to mimic the behavior. I could pretend well enough. Keep my voice steady. Keep my expression calm. Not make a scene. Just play the role.

But inside, I hated it. I hated him.

Before, I had no reason to. He was just one of the boys I trained with. Not close, not distant. Neutral. But now, something in me turned away. The history. The duel. The unfairness of it all. And something else, deeper, like a whisper beneath my skin that screams: Stay away from this man.

Cale left us then, thankfully. As soon as he turned away, Kaelira stepped close and gently took my hand. She looked at me and said nothing aloud, but I understood everything.

Don't worry. I have your back. I felt the warmth of her grip, the steadiness of her presence.

Was this our bond? My magic? Was I, unknowingly, pressing my emotions onto her?

The moment passed, but the feeling lingered.

Not long after, the hostess reappeared and invited everyone to take their places at the dining table. As the room shifted into motion, Cale returned from somewhere behind us. He stepped forward and offered his arm to me with a shallow smile, palm turned outward in a formal gesture. I hated it. I hated him. I couldn’t stand the thought of touching him.

I hesitated, caught in that impossible space between decorum and self-preservation, wondering what choices I had that wouldn't cause a scene.

And then, like a breath of relief, Therrin stepped forward.

He offered his arm without a word, just a steady, patient gesture. I took it, perhaps too quickly. Elsin let out a little laugh, and Serenya followed it with a soft, delighted "Aww."

But I didn’t care. I was just grateful. Perhaps I was overreacting. What would it have hurt to take Cale’s arm? Nothing, maybe. But I was grateful all the same.

Therrin guided me to the dining hall with quiet grace. He didn’t speak much, but his presence felt like a shield. When we arrived, he helped me to my seat before taking his own. A small gesture, but new to me. I had never experienced such attention before. Part of it felt unfamiliar. But part of it was... nice.

When he sat beside me, I let out a breath I hadn’t realized I was holding. Relieved.
