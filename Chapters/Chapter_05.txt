Chapter 5

The fight was over, but the result was not yet settled.

No sooner had Cale hit the ground than his father and one of the Council mages rushed into the ring. They knelt beside him, checking his pulse, brushing back his hair. The mage laid a hand on Cale’s chest and closed his eyes. A long pause.

"He lives," the mage said at last. "But he’s out of Veil."

Veil?

I blinked. What?

Had he used magic? In the ring? During a sanctioned duel?

My mind raced. He hadn’t manifested. We had all been tested. All four of us. The stones had shown nothing.

Whispers began almost instantly.

How did he channel the Veil?

Had he hidden an affinity all this time?

Was he tested falsely?

I should have been focused, but all I felt was a flood of relief. I had escaped. I had won. I would not become the Veilbound. I was sorry for Kaelen, of course. He had no choice in this.

But I had been given a way out. And I had taken it.

Then guilt followed.

I was closer to Kaelen than any of the others. I had trained with him, bled beside him, seen the way he carried himself when no one else was watching. Cale hated him. He made no secret of it. How could he be the one to stand beside Kaelen in the hardest thing he’d ever do?

I would stand at a distance, I told myself. I would protect him from afar. I would keep them both safe.

When we returned to the royal court, Cale was already seated and breathing steadily. A fresh bandage across his ribs.

But then the attention shifted.

Everyone was looking at me.

At first, I thought it was some mistake. Then I saw the mage step forward again.

"During the final match," he said, "Cale manifested an affinity. Windsway.

"It happened during the moment of his recovery, when he surged with speed. That surge was Veilflow. The manifestation was confirmed."

The murmurs grew louder. Even the nobles who had remained quiet throughout the duels now leaned in.

"As such," the mage continued, "he is disqualified from candidacy. The Veilbound must not have a disqualifying affinity before the ritual."

He turned his eyes to me.

"Our Veilbound... will be Rynn Maeren."

The room fell into silence.

My heart, which had calmed for a breath, now roared again in my ears.

I was chosen.

---

Despite everything, I was chosen.

Since the announcement

I didn’t want to talk to anyone. I hated everybody.

The king, for his decision. The mages, for their stupid suggestion. My father, for having no power to stop it. The prince, for no reason I could name. My brother, for inheriting everything I was meant to. My mother and sister, because I was about to become like them. And my friends, for escaping a fate I couldn’t.

But you know who I hated the most? Cale. Because he cheated. He tapped into the Veil when none of us were supposed to. He manifested during combat, against the rules, against what was fair. And I had beaten him, cleanly. I was faster, smarter, calmer. I deserved to win. I deserved to walk away free. I had earned my escape, and yet it was taken from me in a single breath.

And worse, I hated myself. For not manifesting earlier. For not being enough. I felt sick. I felt like disappearing.

But then something happened.

As I lay there, eyes closed, drowning in thoughts I didn’t want, I felt something stir inside me. A small light. A flicker. Just something to focus on. I reached toward it, desperate for calm, any calm at all.

It met me.

It was warm, and cold, at the same time. Strange and steady. My panic melted, my shame quieted.

The storm in my chest cleared, and I felt... still.

The light faded slowly. Not gone. Just quiet.

And I passed into sleep.
