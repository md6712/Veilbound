Chapter 20

Inside the carriage, the conversation shifted.

The laughter from the riverside had faded, replaced now with softer voices and slower tones.

The rhythm of wheels against dirt provided a gentle backdrop as the carriage rocked, drawing us deeper into the hills.

Serenya was the first to steer the conversation. She leaned forward with a mischievous little grin, glancing toward Lady Maerel. "You know, my mother once told me that Lady Maerel was once the most admired girl in all of Elaria. The Pearl of the West, they used to say."

Lady Maerel, seated nearest the window, raised a brow but said nothing at first. She sipped her tea calmly.

"She had over twenty offers," Serenya continued, clearly delighted by the shift in energy.

"Even a prince from Marador tried to court her. Didn’t he send a boat made of gold-leafed wood down the river just to impress you?"

Maerel finally set down her cup. "It was mostly for himself. I had no interest in a man who polished mirrors more than swords."

Elsin laughed. "You broke a few hearts, I imagine."

Maerel tilted her head. "Only the ones foolish enough to offer them."

The laughter drifted again. I sat beside Elsin, so close our skirts overlapped at the hem.

The edge of my dress rested lightly across hers. When the carriage shifted slightly, her bare arm brushed mine. It happened more than once. A soft graze, warm, unbothered. And yet I didn’t feel anything.

Not quite.

Once, I had longed for her. Distant, hopeless, a silent flame. And now she was right beside me. Close enough to smell her perfume, to hear the way she breathed between words. She was beautiful. She had always been beautiful. But now that I was next to her, part of her world, I felt nothing but stillness.

Was my sexuality gone?

My body had changed, completely. My shape, my chemistry. Even my touch felt different. Was this what transformation did? Did it dull desire, or just bury it beneath confusion?

The same is perhaps true for Kaelira. The prince she once was had looked at girls the way I did. He used to make quiet jokes about passing beauties. He never obsessed, but I remembered how his eyes would follow someone across a hall. Now, she sat silent. Watching. Saying nothing.

I wondered if she felt the same thing I did. This strange detachment. This sense of being emotionally underwater.

Perhaps it was the unfamiliarity. Perhaps it was fear. We were given bodies we didn’t choose, thrown into danger we didn’t ask for. Anything could happen. We could be attacked. Killed. Or worse. We couldn’t even protect ourselves the way we once did. We had been warriors. Now, we were escorted symbols. Dressed in silk. Wrapped in elegance. Fragile.

Who could think of romance in such a world?

And yet, here we were.

The conversation danced on. Not quite romance, not directly. But it circled the edge.

"Sir Alric rides well," Elsin said, brushing back her hair with a slow smile. "I’ve been watching him since the crossing. There’s something about the way he holds himself, like he’s always listening for a storm that hasn’t yet come. I wouldn’t mind being the one to bring it."

I knew exactly who she meant. Sir Alric, from the Iron Wall. The one with the scar across his jaw and the steady, haunted eyes. I had watched him, too, once. Admired him. Even imagined, in a foolish way, what it might be like to fight beside him. Now, Elsin had her eye on him.

And I was just beside her. My skirt draped over hers. Her arm brushes mine. And still, I felt... quiet.

Serenya leaned closer to Kaelira and sighed with something between affection and frustration.

"Eryndor is so frustratingly composed. But when he looks at me ... really looks ... I feel like I can hear the air itself go quiet."

Kaelira smiled softly. "He loves you. I would know. He's my brother. I probably know him better than anyone."

Serenya flushed. "We aren’t married yet. The court insists we wait another season. But there’s still that spark."

The carriage rocked slightly again. Outside, trees gave way to open fields. Light filtered in through the curtain, making everything soft. Intimate.

Elsin turned her curiosity on Kaelira. "Your Grace, how do you feel about the prince you're about to bed?"

The question silenced the space for a breath. Kaelira blinked, startled not just by the bluntness, but by the sudden shift in focus.

She recovered slowly, offering a small smile. "You don’t need to be formal," she said gently.

"Not in private. Just Kaelira is fine."

She glanced at each of us in turn, then folded her hands in her lap. "As for how I feel... I don’t know if I feel much of anything. I think I’m too shocked to feel anything clearly. How many days in your life do you wake up to find you’ve been changed? Completely. Your body, your voice, the shape of your hands. Not only that, you’re told you must travel to a foreign court and marry a stranger you've never seen. And on the way, there are people trying to kill you. Or worse. Maybe only Rinya understands how that feels."

Her voice dropped a note. "It’s overwhelming. That’s the best word for it. This isn’t something anyone prepares for. I feel like someone trying to stand on shifting sand."

The answer brought a moment of silence among everyone. Even the laughter and warmth seemed to fold inward, respectful of the weight behind her words.

After a short pause, Serenya shifted toward me, her eyes bright with teasing. "Speaking of princes and protectors, I think Therrin might believe he’s already wed to you, Rinya. The way he fusses around you. Holding your hand, leading you to the blankets, hovering like a shadow every time you move."

I groaned softly and gave her a look. "Serenya."

She only laughed, undeterred. "I’m just saying, I’ve seen how he looks at you. Like a knight in a romance ballad. All noble intent and stolen glances."

"He’s just... helping," I muttered. "We’ve been friends for too long."

"Helping," she echoed, with that dramatic lilt only a sister could master. "Yes, helping you so closely, he might trip over your hem."

The others chuckled. Even Maerel allowed a small smile. Elsin covered her mouth with her fingers, and Kaelira let out the softest laugh, a little more real than any she had offered that day.

"You’re impossible," I said to Serenya, but my lips betrayed me with the hint of a grin.

"And you’re flustered," she replied, victorious.
