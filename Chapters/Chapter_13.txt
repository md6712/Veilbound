Chapter 13

The king and queen sat upon their twin thrones, each elevated and draped in banners of Elaria’s gold and deep navy. To their sides, arranged in a graceful semicircle, were smaller seats for the royal family.

Prince Eryndor sat upright, as stoic as ever, both hands clasped over the hilt of his sword cane. Next to him, Serenya, composed, noble, her smile modest but proud. A few seats over sat the Crown Prince, Edren, with his wife, Princess Alerya of Everis. She wore the sigil of her homeland, a silver rose pinned over sapphire silk.

And then, beside the queen, sat Kaelira.

She looked stunning and overwhelmed. Her pink and white gown shimmered with soft movement as she shifted slightly in her chair and folded her hands in her lap. She didn’t speak.

Didn’t move much. But the way she held herself, like glass warmed by candlelight, drew the room’s full attention.

I was seated beside her.

My breath was shallow. I barely dared raise my head. But I could hear them. Whispered voices drifted in waves from the assembled crowd.

"Is it truly them? I heard they were once boys..."

"Radiant creatures now, like veiled spirits made flesh. Look at the poise... the elegance."

"Surely touched by the Veil itself."

The words pricked like thorns, too sharp and too sweet all at once.

The great hall was full. Lords and ladies filled the space in formal procession, most still standing. The nobles formed elegant clusters, colors of a dozen provinces sewn into silks and brocades.

The mages of the Council stood nearest the thrones. Their robes, plainer than the nobles’, carried more weight. They formed a quiet wall behind the king and queen, Miranda among them, calm, watchful.

To one side of the dais, my father stood among the generals. All in uniform, gleaming metals on deep velvet. Strategists. Veterans. Men of steel and map.

My mother was across from them, near the ladies of the court. She was surrounded by nobles whose names I half-knew, duchesses and baronesses, matrons and widows. All eyes forward.

I noticed a small group at the hall’s edge, my comrades. Therrin. Nerran. Marek. Joval. Deren.

Even Serin. And Cale.

I didn’t dare look too long. I didn’t want to see what they thought of us. Of me.

Then the king rose.

His voice echoed through the hall, calm but firm. "Lords and ladies of Elaria. Honored guests of the Council. Warriors of our realm. Tonight, we bear witness to a miracle of courage. The sacrifice made by Princess Kaelira and Lady Rinya shall not be forgotten."

He paused, glancing at the two of us.

"This act, made not for glory, but for peace, has altered the course of our history. And for that, we owe them not only our admiration, but our protection."

A respectful murmur passed through the court.

"To the mages of the Council," the king continued, "who risked much to make this possible, and to our loyal guards and generals, who stood watch when danger came, we give our deepest thanks."

Danger?

He nodded solemnly. "In these past days, two attempts were made to sabotage this moment. Both were swiftly put down thanks to your vigilance."

A hush fell over the room.

"And now, let it be known: the Princess and her Veilbound companion shall depart for Veylan at the end of this week."

There was a stir among the gathered nobles.

"Their journey is sacred. Their safety, paramount."

Then, looking directly at the group of my comrades, the king declared, "To ensure this, the Princess and Lady Rinya shall be accompanied not only by her trusted companions, but by an escort of honored knights."

A knot twisted in my chest. These were the friends of my old life, the witnesses of my unraveling, and now, somehow, they were to be the guards of my new one. I didn’t know whether to feel honored, afraid, or betrayed by the weight of it.

He truly meant for us to walk together again.

Then the king lifted his hand, and a herald stepped forward with a scroll. One by one, the names were called.

"Therrin of House Rennev."

Therrin stepped forward with his usual steady gait, head high but eyes warm. A murmur moved through the court, he had always been considered a promising fighter, and now his loyalty was being recognized.

"Cale of House Rennar."

Cale followed next. I didn’t look at him directly. I couldn’t. But I felt his presence as clearly as if he were touching my shoulder. The hall grew tense, unsure how to respond. Some clapped. Others were silent.

"Nerran of House Yvane."

Nerran was eager, but today he bowed with restraint. Not too proud. Not too meek.

"Joval of House Enmar."

He gave a sweeping bow, theatrical as ever. A few nobles chuckled. The tension eased.

"Marek of House Vance."

Marek stepped forward with precise, unreadable control.

"Deren of House Korr."

Deren followed, quieter than usual, but his posture held pride.

"Serin of House Teyne."

He was the last and the youngest. He bowed low and took his place.

The seven of them now stood before the throne, shoulder to shoulder.

The king nodded once. "These are the shields of our future. Let none question their honor."

Then the king turned to Prince Eryndor. "Eryndor, come forth."

Eryndor rose without hesitation and stepped forward, his cane tapping once against the polished stone floor. The king met his son’s gaze and said, "They are yours to command. Until their journey ends, you shall oversee their safety."

A ripple of surprise moved through the room. Though not part of the original escort, Eryndor was now publicly entrusted as the commander of the royal guard unit protecting Princess Kaelira.

Then the king nodded to the herald again.

"Sir Alvis of the Royal Guard," the herald called.

The seasoned knight stepped forward, his silver-threaded cape flowing behind him. He bowed deeply.

"Sir Tavren of House Aleran. Sir Mollian of the Sapphire Order."

Each name echoed through the hall, and with it, a figure emerged, knights known across Elaria for valor and discipline.

Then, two mages stepped forward from the Council's line. The first was Miranda, composed as ever in her deep blue robe. She folded her hands and offered a serene nod to Kaelira and me.

Beside her stood a tall man in silver and green, his eyes thoughtful, Mage Halren, a Veilwarden from the Northern Sanctum.

"We will accompany the Princess," Miranda said.
