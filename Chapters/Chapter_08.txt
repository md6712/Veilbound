Chapter 8

The next few days felt like imprisonment. Prince Kaelen and I were kept under the strictest watch, more guarded than royalty during wartime. The fear of sabotage was real. No one took chances. We weren’t allowed outside without escort. Our rooms were sealed. Meals were controlled. Every movement monitored.

But it wasn’t just about keeping us alive. It was about preparation.

Every morning, and then again in the afternoon, we trained under the two Council mages, Miranda and her colleague. They guided us through veil flow exercises, helping us learn to sense, contain, and redirect the magic moving through our bodies.

There was something else we were working on too, something more delicate and harder to describe. Once we learned to stabilize the Veil inside ourselves, we had to begin practicing how to share it. To link. This was the first step toward becoming Veilbound.

It wasn’t a spell. It was a relationship. A rhythm.

Veilbound required harmony. And the bond between us had to be forged before the ritual, or the Veil would snap.

Time was our enemy.

The mages were visibly relieved that I had manifested Stillmark. If I hadn’t, the entire burden would have fallen on Kaelen to control both flows, his and mine. That would have made things dangerously unstable.

But now, with Stillmark, I could help. My affinity, born to soothe, stabilize, and suppress emotional volatility, was almost tailor-made for this task. If I focused, if I listened, I could guide both of us toward balance.

So most of the time, when we weren’t eating or sleeping, we trained.

We sat cross-legged in mirrored positions, hands on the floor, breathing in rhythm. We walked in circles, eyes closed, trying to sense each other’s presence through the Veil. We practiced until our veins buzzed with magic and our thoughts blurred into one continuous flow of fatigue.

We said little. Not because we were angry, but because we were afraid. Afraid of failing, afraid of bonding, afraid of what would come after.

In my heart, I was hoping we never bonded. If we didn’t, I wouldn’t truly become the Veilbound. Some small part of me even considered sabotaging the connection, just enough to be set free. But that would have been betrayal. Betrayal to my country. To my friend, who was sacrificing his identity, his body. And to myself. It would have been a lie, and worse than becoming a woman, I think. So I couldn’t. I wouldn’t.

So I genuinely tried. I gave it everything. And I believe Kaelen did too. Even though we barely spoke, even though our eyes rarely met, we trained like it was our duty. Because it was. Because it needed focus, not feelings.

On the third day, we finally bonded.

The mages guided us carefully. The flow of Veil between us stabilized, synchronized. Miranda instructed Kaelen to reach toward my inner source. I was told to let him in.

The moment he found it, I felt a sharp internal ache. It was unmistakable. I was bound.

Then something unexpected happened. The prince looked at me with calm intensity, and I felt a pull. Through the Veil, he urged me to search for his source, even though that wasn’t part of the mage’s plan. I hesitated. I was supposed to be the one bound, not the other way around. But the feeling was clear. A request.

The mages noticed something amiss, shifting in their stances, but Kaelen’s expression was resolute. He didn’t speak. He didn’t have to. I reached, and I found his source. Our flows braided. We were bonded.

The mage explained it softly, taking a long breath before speaking. When we were close to each other, our Veilflows would now naturally intertwine. We would be able to share strength, stabilize one another’s casting, and even recover faster from Veil strain. It was more than a magical link, it was a synchronization of soul and purpose.

He smiled faintly. "You were once like comrades. Now, you are something closer. Soon, twin sisters in form as well as spirit."
