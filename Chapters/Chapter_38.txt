Chapter 38

sorry for the delayed update. I try to update more often as we enter spring and summer. The road to the Veylan capital rose slowly beneath us, wide and worn from generations of travel.

Even before we saw the city itself, we felt its presence.

The air changed.

It carried weight, expectation, something vast waiting just beyond the horizon.

And yet, my thoughts were not entirely there.

They kept drifting back to the three companions we had the last few days.

Judew, Pattin, Sedrick.

The way they had appeared out of nowhere. The way they had fought. The masks. The fear in the guards’ eyes when Judew’s face was revealed. It all pressed at my mind, refusing to settle.

Who were they, truly?

Not simple travelers. Not mere mercenaries. That much was certain.

And why had they helped us?

I could still feel the echo of Kaelira’s blush through the Veilbond, the softness that had lingered after his farewell, and it tangled strangely with my own unease. Something about them had not been finished. Not resolved.

We had left them behind.

But I had the quiet, unsettling sense that they had not left us.

Our caravan moved in tighter formation now. Banners were raised. Armor was adjusted. Even the horses seemed to sense the shift, stepping with a steadier, more deliberate rhythm.

Beside me, Kaelira sat with careful practice, though through the Veilbond I felt the tension coiled inside her. Anticipation. Fear. Something heavier that neither of us had words for. Across from us, Lady Maerel had joined our carriage. She sat with folded hands, watching us with a calm, measuring gaze and offering the occasional quiet correction when our posture slipped.

We crested the final ridge.

And there it was.

The capital of Veylan.

Stone walls stretched wide across the land, impossibly high, layered in tiers that caught the light of the afternoon sun. Towers rose like spears into the sky, each crowned with dark banners that snapped sharply in the wind. The gates alone were massive, tall enough to swallow entire companies of soldiers without slowing.

Beyond them, the city spread outward in dense waves of rooftops, smoke curling into the sky, the distant hum of thousands of lives blending into a single restless sound.

I had never seen anything like it.

No one spoke for a long moment.

Even the knights seemed quieter.

“This…” Kaelira whispered beside me. “This is where we go.”

Where we will stay. Where everything changes. The caravan began its descent.

As we moved closer, the world around the gates came alive.

At first it was only a few scattered carts and figures along the road. Then more appeared.

Then dozens.

A crowd had gathered before the city.

Farmers with wagons piled high with grain, vegetables, and bundled reeds. Merchants with covered carts, crates tied down with rope, some calling out prices even before reaching the gates. Travelers on foot, cloaked and dusty, waiting their turn to pass inside.

The closer we came, the thicker it grew. This city was far larger than Elaris, the capital of my homeland, Elaria. Its scale swallowing everything around it, even before we had passed its gates.

Lines formed. Voices rose. Animals shifted and snorted in the press of bodies. The scent of earth, sweat, hay, and trade filled the air.

And then they noticed us. At first only a few heads turned. Then more.

Our banners. Our formation. The polished armor of our knights. The guarded carriages. Whispers began to spread.

From inside the carriage, I watched through the narrow slit of the curtain, my now slender fingers resting lightly against the fabric. It felt strange, deeply strange, to be the one observed instead of the one observing.

They stared. Gossip moves fast among folk, they must have heard of our arrival.

Curious. Wary. Some bowed their heads. Others simply watched, eyes wide, measuring us as we passed.

I shifted slightly, instinctively straightening my posture, adjusting the fall of my sleeves the way the ladies had taught me. Kaelira did the same beside me, her chin lifting just enough to carry the weight of her title.

“This feels…” I murmured, unsure how to finish the thought.

“Different,” Kaelira replied.

It was.

We were no longer travelers moving unseen along quiet roads.

But neither were we fully revealed.

We were half-hidden behind curtains and silk, guarded from view, yet known. People wanted to see us, I could feel that clearly, but they were given only glimpses. Shadows behind fabric.

Movement behind drawn cloth.

And for that, I was grateful.

I was not ready to be seen. Not like this. Not yet.
